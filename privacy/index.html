Privacy

Veila is a static website. There are no accounts, no logins, and no forms that ask for your name or email. This page lists everything the site does with data — it is short, because there is not much.

Your readings stay on your device

When you draw cards on Veila, the reading happens in your browser. The cards you draw and the readings you see are not sent to or stored on any server. If the site remembers anything between visits — your language choice, a reading in progress — it is kept in your browser's local storage, on your device, and you can clear it at any time through your browser settings.

Analytics

Veila uses Google Analytics 4 to understand how the site is used: which pages are visited, which reading questions are chosen, and which buttons are clicked. These events are anonymous usage signals — they are not tied to a name, an email, or anything you type, because the site never asks for any of those. Google Analytics sets cookies and processes data under Google's privacy policy.

Advertising

Some pages show ads served by Google AdSense. Google and its partners may use cookies to show ads based on your prior visits to this or other websites. You can adjust how Google personalises ads at Google Ads Settings.

Hosting

The site is served as static files by GitHub Pages. Like most web hosts, GitHub may record standard technical logs (such as IP addresses) to run and secure the service, as described in GitHub's privacy statement. Veila itself runs no server and keeps no logs.

What Veila never collects

No names, no email addresses, no phone numbers, no payment details, no account passwords — the site has no way to collect them, because none of those fields exist. Questions you reflect on privately while reading are yours alone; the site only records which pre-written question page or reading you opened, as an anonymous analytics event.

Contact

Veila is an independent project with no support desk or mailing list. To know more about who makes the site and how the readings are written, see the about page.
