Six of Swords

The Voyager

Six of Swords — Upright

transition, escaping trouble, healing, moving on, gradual peace

You are in the 'in-between' phase—drifting away from rough waters toward something calmer. It feels quiet and maybe a bit sad, but you are safe now.

Six of Swords — Reversed

Inability to move on, Unfinished business, Failed transition, Stuck in the past, Returning to trouble

You're rowing in circles. The same old problems are bubbling up again because the emotional baggage is still on the boat. You can't move forward until you deal with it.

In the Celtic Cross spread

How Six of Swords reads in each of the ten Celtic Cross positions.

I. The Present

Upright

You are currently in a state of transition, moving away from a turbulent mental space toward calmer, more logical waters. It is a time of quiet departure and necessary recovery.

Reversed

You are currently in a state of emotional gridlock, trying to move toward calmer waters but feeling as though the boat is stuck in the reeds.

II. The Challenge

Upright

The challenge lies in the emotional baggage you’re still carrying into the new chapter. It’s hard to paddle forward when you’re still looking back at the shore you’ve left behind.

Reversed

The challenge is a heavy piece of baggage you refuse to throw overboard; something from the past is anchoring you to the very place you’re trying to leave.

III. The Past

Upright

You have recently made the difficult decision to leave a stressful situation, and that momentum of 'moving on' is what has brought you to this current calm.

Reversed

A recent attempt to escape or move on was stalled or incomplete, leaving you in this halfway point between where you were and where you want to be.

IV. The Future

Upright

The path ahead leads to a more stable environment where the fog lifts and you can finally think without the interference of high-intensity emotions.

Reversed

The path ahead involves a forced confrontation with your unresolved issues; the journey cannot continue until the leaks in the boat are patched.

V. Above · Conscious

Upright

Consciously, you are seeking clarity and planning a logical exit strategy. You are focused on finding a practical solution to a long-standing problem.

Reversed

Your mind is fixated on the logistics of change or travel, perhaps over-analyzing why the transition feels so difficult and slow.

VI. Below · Unconscious

Upright

Subconsciously, there is a deep, weary longing for peace and a need to escape a cycle that has drained your mental energy for far too long.

Reversed

Deep down, there is a subconscious resistance to the unknown destination; you might actually prefer the familiar discomfort to the uncertainty of a new shore.

VII. Advice · Self

Upright

You are acting as the quiet navigator of your own life, choosing to move in silence rather than stay and fight a battle that can't be won.

Reversed

You feel weary and unmotivated, perhaps acting as the reluctant passenger in your own life, waiting for someone else to take the oars.

VIII. External Influences

Upright

Your current surroundings provide a safe passage or a bridge to your next destination, often involving people who are helping you move without asking for much in return.

Reversed

The current environment is plagued by delays, miscommunication, or logistical hiccups that make physical or emotional movement feel nearly impossible.

IX. Hopes & Fears

Upright

You hope for a fresh start in a place where things make sense, but you fear that the sadness of what you left behind will follow you forever.

Reversed

You hope for a clean break and a fresh start, but you fear that you are carrying your troubles with you no matter how far you sail.

X. Outcome

Upright

The situation is likely to resolve in a successful transition; you will reach a mental shore that offers the perspective and distance you’ve been needing.

Reversed

If you remain stalled, the situation will stagnate in a restless gray zone; progress only returns once you acknowledge what you are truly afraid to leave behind.
